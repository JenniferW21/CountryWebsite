Chinese New Years

Chinese New Year is the most important holiday in China.
CNY celebrates the end of the old year and the start of a new one.
The Chinese New Year animals change annually on a twelve year cycle.
CNY is usually celebrates between January 21 and February 20.

Dragon Boat Festival

This Festival is to honor Qu Yuan who gave his life for the country.
The origins of Dragon Boat Festival has over 2,000 years of history.
It occurs on the fifth day of the fifth month on the Chinese calendar.
The boat itslef are long, narrow dragons that are used during dragon boat races.

Mid-Autumn Festival

Mid-Autumn Festival is a harvest festival celebrated in Chinese culture.
The festival orginated from the worship of the moon and has over 2,000 years of history.
It is celebrated during a full moon, correspomding to mid-September to ealry October.
It is a festival for mooncakes as it symbolizes family unity and prosperity.

Chinese Opera

Chinese Opera is a form of musical theatre.
The performer would wear face paint to protray roles.
Action, music, and dance conveys a story often based on events.
Chinese Opera orginated from early form of Chinese drama.
